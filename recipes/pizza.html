Margharita Pizza

Description

A Margherita pizza is a classic Italian dish known for its simplicity and fresh, high-quality ingredients. It features a thin, crisp yet slightly chewy crust, traditionally baked in a wood-fired oven to achieve a perfect balance of crunchy and tender. The base of the pizza is lightly coated with a vibrant tomato sauce, made from ripe, crushed tomatoes that are seasoned with salt, and sometimes garlic and herbs, creating a rich and slightly tangy foundation.

ingredients

  • Pizza dough
  • Ripe tomatoes or high-quality canned tomato sauce
  • Fresh mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to its highest setting, ideally with a pizza stone inside.
  2. Roll out pizza dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness.
  3. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the dough.
  4. Tear fresh mozzarella into small pieces and distribute evenly on top of the sauce.
  5. Add fresh basil leaves and a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil.
  6. Season with salt, and optionally, minced garlic, black pepper, or Italian seasoning.
  7. Transfer the pizza to the preheated pizza stone and bake until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly, usually about 5-10 minutes, depending on your oven.
  8. Slice and serve hot.
